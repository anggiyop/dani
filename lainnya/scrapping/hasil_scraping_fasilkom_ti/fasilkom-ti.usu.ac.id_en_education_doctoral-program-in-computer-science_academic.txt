Learning Outcomes

Doctoral students in Computer Science receive computer science education so that they are able to create updates from the most recent developments in the field of computer science, compile scientific papers and publish them in internationally reputable journals, solve complex problems in the field of computer science through inter, multi, and transdisciplinary approaches, are able to follow and respond to developments in the field of computer science, and are able to lead in the field of computer science and informatics

Learning Outcomes
1	Respect God Almighty, uphold religious values, morals, and professional ethics, abide by the law, have a sense of patriotism and the duty to contribute to the nation and state, and respect the law.
2	Sincere, independent, professionally competent, and socially aware
3	Having a personality of leadership and being able to conduct ‘ socially transdisciplinary research in the field of computer science.
4	Capable of constructing scientific arguments using scientific concepts and generating ideas, methods, and tools in the field of computer science to assist new discoveries in computer science and technology, particularly for artificial intelligence, the Internet of things, computer networks and data communication, and machine learning development.
5	Capable of developing a computer science research roadmap based on the most recent technology and scientific advancements
6	Capable of imparting knowledge in the field of computer science for the community's benefit.
7	Capable of providing problem-solving services by employing inter-, multi-, or transdisciplinary
