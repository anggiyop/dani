Tjong Yong Hian Garden

Tjong Yong Hian Garden, often dubbed the Chinese-style Flower Garden, is an impressive tribute to Tjong Yong Hian, brother of the famous Tjong A Fie. Both are influential figures in the history of Medan. The flower garden is a testament to the harmonious relationship between different religions and cultures that has graced Medan City over the years and showcases Tjong Yong Hian's treasured legacy. Not to be missed, the graves of Tjong Yong Hian and his wife are located within the garden. This proximity provides an emotionally moving personal touch for visitors. Not only that, but the inscriptions within the park are also eye-catching, with Arabic, Dutch, and Chinese inscriptions recounting Tjong Yong Hian's life journey and achievements. Stepping into every corner of the garden, visitors will be spoiled by the stunning panorama. Despite its modest size, the Tjong Yong Hian Flower Garden boasts a Chinese style like no other. Every element and design is carefully selected to create an authentic and alluring atmosphere. The garden invites visitors daily from 8am to 5pm, free of charge. An unmissable opportunity to enjoy the beauty and charm of a garden that captivates the heart and delights the eyes.

Other Information

Tjong Yong Hian Park is often dubbed as the Chinese-style Flower Park that was built as a form of respect for the figure of Tjong Yong Hian, one of the influential people in the history of Medan city.
