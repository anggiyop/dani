National Achievement-Based Selection (SNBP) Student Program

About SNBP

SNBP (National Selection Based on Achievement) is a substitute for SNMPTN (National Selection for State Higher Education Institution Entrance), which is no longer held nationally by the Institute for Higher Education Institution Entrance Test (LTMPT) but its role is continued by the Technical Implementation Unit of the Center for Educational Testing Management (BP3). In the SNBP pathway, prospective students are emphasized to have holistic and cross-disciplinary competencies. In the 2024/2025 Academic Year, students who obtain an SMA/MA/SMK diploma and show high academic achievement are entitled to choose to continue their education at Universitas Sumatera Utara (USU) both in the undergraduate and diploma programs. The SNBP registration stages in 2025 are expected to be the same as in previous years, although the details are still subject to change in accordance with the SNBP committee policy. The registration stage begins with filling in the prospective studentsâ€™ private data through the official SNBP website (https://snpmb.bppp.kemdikbud.go.id) and personal data according to the instructions provided. Make sure the data entered is correct and complete. After filling in the personal data, you will enter the education payment stage. Payments can be made through banks or online payment services that have been appointed by the SNBP committee. Follow the procedure that has been told carefully.

Currently, USU provides at least 20% of the total capacity of undergraduate and diploma programs through the SNBP pathway. The 2025 SNBP schedule starts from 28 December 2024 for the process of determining eligible/quota students, then on 06 January - 31 January 2025 filling in PDSS (School and Student Database), then on 04 - 18 Februari 2025 is the SNBP registration period. The selection results will be announced on 18 Maret 2025.

Applicant Requirements

Indonesian citizen (WNI) with a National Identification Number (NIK).
Possesses a NISN and is registered in PDSS.
Has report card grades entered in PDSS according to the requirements.
Must upload a portfolio for those choosing Art & Sports study programs.
Has academic achievements and meets the requirements set by each state university (PTN).

School Requirements

NPSN: SMA/SMK/MA that has an NPSN or National School Identification Number.

Accreditation requirements for student allocation

A Accreditation: Top 40% in their school.

B Accreditation: Top 25% in their school.

C Accreditation and others: Top 5% in their school.

Schools that use e-rapor in the PDSS system will get an additional 5% quota of eligible students.
Fill out the School and Student Database (PPDS). Only eligible student data should be entered according to the requirements.

Requirements

General Terms

SNBP is conducted based on an academic achievement assessment by using report cards and students' academic and non-academic achievements as determined by PTN. The evaluated achievements are the three best achievements.
Schools participating in SNBP must have a National School Registration Number (NPSN) and enter eligible students' report cards in the School and Student Database (PDSS) accurately and completely.
Schools must have a School SNPMB Account to fill in the PDSS, and students must have a Student SNPMB Account to register for SNBP. School SNPMB Account registration and Student SNPMB Account registration are conducted through the SNPMB portal.

Additional Terms

Students who have been declared to have passed the SNBP 2025, SNBP 2024, and SNBP 2023 cannot register for UTBK-SNBT 2025.
Students who are declared to have passed the SNBP 2025 selection cannot register for the Independent selection path at any state university (PTN).

Study Program Options

Every eligible student is allowed to choose a study program at Academic PTN and/or Vocational PTN.
Each student can choose two study programs from one or two PTNs.
If choosing one study program, students can choose a PTN in any province.
If choosing two study programs, one of the study programs must be at a PTN in the same province as their SMA/MA/SMK.
