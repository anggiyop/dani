State Museum of North Sumatra

The State Museum of North Sumatra, located at Jl. HM. Joni No. 51, Teladan Barat, Medan Kota, is a fascinating destination that allows visitors to deeply understand the rich and diverse cultural heritage of the North Sumatra region. With an extensive and diverse collection, including archaeological artifacts, ethnographic objects, fine arts, and natural history, the museum is a window that takes us on a journey into the historical heritage of the archipelago. The State Museum of North Sumatra was established in 1982, but the laying of the first collection inaugurated by President Soekarno took place in 1954. The museum building is an interesting exhibition center, arousing visitors' interest to learn about the rich culture of the past. From ancient artifacts to modern works of art, the museum provides an immersive and engaging experience. In addition, the State Museum of North Sumatra is open to the general public on Tuesday-Sunday, from 9 am to 4 pm. With an affordable entrance ticket of only IDR3,000 per person, you and your friends can enjoy your free time from college or hang out by strolling around here. In addition, you can also look for aesthetic and charming photo spots in this museum. The State Museum of North Sumatra is unmissable for culture lovers and history enthusiasts.
