Muhammad Ernesto Azguevara Ganis Siregar, S.Ked, a student of the Faculty of Medicine, Universitas Sumatera Utara, Doctor Professional Education Program (Co-Ass), won the top 5 in the Menopause Olympic competition. The event was organized by the International Menopause Society (IMS) at the 19th World Congress of Menopause meeting which took place in Melbourne Australia, October 19-22, 2024.

This success was achieved after eliminating 52 participants from 72 countries attending the biennial scientific meeting. The 52 participants initially had to compete with hundreds of participants to be selected through a rigorous selection by the Scientific Committee and were first designated to win Young Scholars Bursary Awards, scholarships funded through the IMS Endowment for Education and Research (EER). Hundreds of participants competed to submit their research or scientific work for selection, and were required to be less than 40 years old on the first day of the congress, be pursuing an accredited degree such as PhD, MD and Masters within the last 5 years, present their work, attend all scientific events and be interested in participating in the Menopause Olympics. The Young Scholar Bursary Awards winners receive accommodation in the form of round-trip departure tickets and lodging costs during the activity from the organizing committee.

On October 19, at 7:30 am local time, a preliminary selection of 52 people was held, resulting in 25 participants who would compete in the Olympic Menopause finals in the afternoon, at approximately 6:00 pm.

After making it to the top 25, Ernesto and the other participants lined up to sit on stage, their backs to the screen watched by all congress participants, and used an application prepared by the organizers to be installed on their cell phones. The questions could be viewed on the stage screen by the audience, while the contestants concentrated on answering each question correctly and faster, led by Professor Tim Hiillard (UK) and Professor Nicole Jaff (South Africa). After competing in the finals for 3 rounds, Ernesto Siregar finally managed to rank 5th out of all participants (the winners were named in the order determined by the top 5 best). This was a surprise for Ernesto considering that he is currently still a co-assistant and has not completed his medical professional education at the USU Faculty of Medicine, while other competitors are residents / PPDS or PhD or Masters candidates.

Ernesto Ganis Siregar is also the Head of USU Football UKM, and is always active in the annual IMT-GT competition. In addition to the Olympic Menopause competition, at the 19th World Congress Menopause-International Menopause Society scientific meeting in Melbourne, several USU Faculty of Medicine lecturers also appeared as speakers who disseminated their research results at the international level. Among them are Dr dr Cut Adeya Adella, Sp.OG Subsp.Onk (Chairperson of TKPPDS FK USU), dr Iman Helmi, Sp.OG Subsp. Obginsos (SPS Obgin FK USU), Dr dr Cut Arya Arina, Sp.N (Lecturer in Neurology FK USU), dr Anditha Sitompul, Sp.OG (USU Hospital staff). Also appearing were several Obgyn PPDS participants from the USU Faculty of Medicine who were required to make international presentations of their scientific work before the national exam to become specialists, including Dr. Akbar Nasution, Dr. Trinidia Lubis, Dr. Putri Wulandari, Dr. Atikah Harun Nasution, Dr. Afrilia Rosada Lubis and Dr. Rizqi Tanjung. Other PTNBHs that attended the scientific presentation were FK UNS Solo (two lecturers and 3 Obgyn PPDS), FK UNIBRAW Malang (one Obgyn lecturer), FK UI (one medical rehabilitation lecturer), and FK UNTAN Pontianak (one lecturer).

Secretary of the Universitas Sumatera Utara, Prof. Dr. Muhammad Fidel Ganis Siregar, M.Ked (OG), Sp.OG, Subsp.F.E.R, hopes that competitions such as the International Menopause Society can be followed by more USU students, considering that all costs are borne by the committee and will certainly add to the USU IKU achievement points.
