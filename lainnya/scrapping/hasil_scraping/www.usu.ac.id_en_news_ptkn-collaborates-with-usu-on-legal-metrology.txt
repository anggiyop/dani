Director General PTKN Collaborates with USU on Legal Metrology
 

Published by

Bambang Riyanto

Published at

Tuesday, 19 July 2022

USU Rector Dr. Muryanto Amin., S.Sos., M.Si., said, after the establishment of this collaboration, USU Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences (FMIPA) Lecturers will prepare and develop a framework for assessment and achievement in order to achieve the main goal of building this partnership, namely to improve the quality of USU graduates while supporting the development of skills, knowledge and innovation of USU graduates, to compete in an increasingly competitive global job market.

PR USU - The Universitas Sumatera Utara (USU) officially cooperates with the Director General of Consumer Protection and Business Order (PTKN) in order to support the implementation of Merdeka Learning Campus Merdeka (MBKM) and Teaching Practitioners in fields related to legal metrology. The activity took place at the Central Administration Bureau Building, on Tuesday (7/12/2022).

USU Rector, Dr. Muryanto Amin., S.Sos., M.Si said that USU Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences (FMIPA) Lecturers will prepare and develop a framework for assessment and achievement in order to achieve the main goal of building this partnership after the establishment of this collaboration, namely to improve the quality of USU graduates while supporting the development of skills, knowledge and innovation of USU graduates, to compete in an increasingly competitive global job market.

"With this collaboration, it is possible to transfer information, expertise and experience from practitioners and professionals to USU students," he concluded.

The Rector also hopes that with this collaboration, the relationship between USU and PTKN can be closer and able to make a positive contribution to both institutions. And it is also hoped that it will further expand the opportunities and opportunities for USU to be faster and on target in achieving the work program that has been targeted.

"I hope that this collaboration will further expand the opportunities and opportunities for USU to be faster and on target in achieving the work program that has been targeted," he said.

Director General of Consumer Protection and Trade Order (PKTN) Very Anggrijono, SE., M.Si said that after this collaboration, PTKN could straighten out the existing issues and students could implement the knowledge gained in the field of legal metrology.

The Director General of PKTN also hopes that with students implementing their knowledge, the public can open their minds to legal metrology and can help the community in their problems. (©ULC)
