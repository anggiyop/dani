About USU

Universitas Sumatera Utara (abbreviated as USU) is a state university in Medan, North Sumatra, Indonesia. USU was initially established with the name Yayasan Universitet Sumatera Utara on June 4, 1952. Our first faculty was the Faculty of Medicine, established on August 20, 1952. President Soekarno inaugurated USU as the 7th State University in Indonesia on November 20, 1957. In 2003, USU changed its university status to status as a Legal Entity State University (PTN-BH) and became the 5th PTN-BH university in Indonesia.

International Affairs Office

The International Affairs Office (IAO) of Universitas Sumatera Utara is a division under the Vice Rector for Planning and Cooperation, which provides expert support and guidance for the formal international relations of Universitas Sumatera Utara. The office contributes to the internationalization of USU through leadership and administrative support. IAO facilitates international collaboration and coordinates activities designed to bring USU closer to the shared vision of USU being internationally recognized for its research, education, and community service.

Access and Transportation

Students from outside Medan can take a flight to Kualanamu Airport and continue their journey to the USU campus by taxi or bus. Students can also take a train and continue their journey using public transportation.

Students can use buses to reach USU by going through the nearest bus terminal and getting off at Amplas Terminal. After that, students can continue their journey to the USU campus using public transportation, which takes approximately 30 minutes.

Students can reach the location of Universitas Sumatera Utara by taking a flight. After arriving at Kualanamu Airport, the journey can be continued by taking a taxi or bus, which takes approximately 60 minutes.

Students can use a ship and dock at Belawan Port. Then, the journey can be continued by public transportation or bus to the location of USU, which takes approximately 120 minutes.
